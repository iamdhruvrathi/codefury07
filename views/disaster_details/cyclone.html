Cyclone Overview

Understand the nature of cyclones, their effects, and how to stay safe during such events.

What is a Cyclone?

A cyclone is a powerful storm system with a low-pressure center, strong winds, and heavy rainfall. It often causes significant damage, especially in coastal areas.

Formation and Development

How Cyclones Form

Cyclones develop over warm ocean waters (≥ 26.5°C/80°F) where moist air rises, creating a low-pressure area. This rising air starts to spin due to the Earth’s rotation (Coriolis effect), leading to cyclone formation.

Growth and Structure

The cyclone intensifies as it draws in warm air and releases energy. It forms a calm eye at the center, surrounded by an eyewall with the most intense weather.

Potential Hazards

Strong Winds

Wind speeds in cyclones can exceed 200 km/h (124 mph), causing severe damage to infrastructure and vegetation.

Storm Surge

Storm surge pushes ocean water onto land, resulting in flooding and potential destruction in coastal regions.

Heavy Rainfall

Intense rain can lead to flash flooding and landslides, especially in elevated areas.

Impact on Regions in India

Eastern Coast

States like Odisha, West Bengal, Andhra Pradesh, and Tamil Nadu are prone to cyclones.

Western Coast

Gujarat and Maharashtra can experience cyclones, though less frequently than the east coast.

Safety Measures

Preparation

  • Monitor weather updates and warnings from reliable sources like the IMD.
  • Secure your home and prepare an emergency kit with essentials.
  • Plan for evacuation if needed.

During a Cyclone

  • Stay indoors in a safe area away from windows.
  • Follow local authorities’ instructions.

After a Cyclone

  • Avoid areas with floodwater and check for structural damage.
  • Inspect your home for safety hazards before re-entering.

Emergency Kit Essentials

  • Water: At least 3 liters per person per day for three days.
  • Non-perishable Food: Sufficient for at least three days.
  • First Aid Kit: Include bandages, antiseptics, and prescription medications.
  • Flashlight and Batteries: For power outages.
  • Important Documents: Store in a waterproof container.
  • Multi-tool: For various emergency needs.
  • Clothing and Blankets: To keep warm and protected.
  • Sanitation Supplies: Wet wipes, hand sanitizer, and plastic bags.

Historical Cyclone Events

Cyclone Fani (2019)

This cyclone caused extensive damage in Odisha, affecting millions and resulting in significant casualties and property loss.

Cyclone Amphan (2020)

One of the most powerful cyclones to hit West Bengal, leading to severe destruction in Kolkata and surrounding areas.

Recognizing Cyclone Signs

  • Weather Changes: Watch for sudden drops in pressure, increased humidity, darkening skies, and strong winds.
  • Forecasts: Pay attention to updates and warnings from meteorological departments.
